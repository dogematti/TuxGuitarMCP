# Metalcore player notes (7-string A standard: A1 D2 G2 C3 F3 A3 D4)

Starter template written from the user's own vocabulary - edit freely.
Everything here is served to the AI alongside the built-in rubric, and
wins over the generic recipe when they conflict.

## Fretboard vocabulary

- Heavy chug lives on the OPEN low A string (string 7): 0-0-0-0 patterns,
  palm muted. The open string is the engine; fretted chug is the exception.
- Heavy riffing on the D string (string 6): frets 5-7-8
  (= G2, A2, Bb2 - the b7, root, b2 colors).
- Melodic riff range on the G string (string 5): frets 5-7-8-10-12
  (= C3, D3, Eb3, F3, G3 - b3, 4, b5, b6, b7 against A).
- Octave stabs (dissonant metalcore color): string 5 + string 3 shapes,
  slide them; do not sit on one position.

## Riff shapes

- Verse riffs: open-A chug groups broken by single D-string or G-string
  notes on the accents - the fretted note IS the hook.
- Breakdown: open low A only, rhythm does all the work; leave at least
  one half-beat hole per bar. Kick in unison always.
- The b2 (Bb = D string fret 8, or low A string fret 1) is the money
  note - use it at phrase ends, not constantly.

## House rules

- Palm mute everything on strings 6-7 except accents.
- No solos over breakdowns.
- If a riff has no open low A in it, it is probably not a metalcore riff
  in this tuning.
